Global Insights

The Cost of Compute: A \$7 Trillion Race to Scale Data Centres

AI is fuelling a massive demand for compute power, driving billions in infrastructure investment, but future demand remains uncertain. By 2030, data centers globally are projected to require \$6.7 trillion in capital expenditures, with \$5.2 trillion specifically for AI-equipped data centers. Companies face the challenge of balancing rapid capital deployment with prudence due to uncertainty about future AI growth. Overinvestment risks stranding assets, while underinvestment means falling behind.

Key Projections & Scenarios:

  • Base Scenario: 156 incremental GW added by 2030, requiring \$5.2 trillion.
  • Accelerated Demand: 205 incremental GW, requiring \$7.9 trillion.
  • Constrained Demand: 78 incremental GW, requiring \$3.7 trillion.

Investor Archetypes & Capex Distribution:

  • Technology Developers (~60%): \$3.1 trillion for chips and hardware.
  • Energizers (~25%): \$1.3 trillion for power generation and cooling.
  • Builders (~15%): \$0.8 trillion for land and site development.

India Mobile Broadband Index 2025

India has emerged as a major 5G success story. Mobile data traffic jumped 23% year-over-year, touching 21.5 EB per month in 2024. 5G data traffic is expected to surpass 4G by Q1 2026.

Key Highlights:

  • 5G Traffic: Pan-India monthly 5G traffic surged threefold year-over-year.
  • Data per User: Average monthly data per user soared to 27.5 GB in 2024.
  • 5G Devices: Active 5G devices doubled to reach 271 million in 2024.
  • 5G FWA: Fixed Wireless Access is a key catalyst, with FWA users consuming over 12 times more data than mobile users.

Signalling the Future: Five Telco Trends in 2025

The telecommunications industry is set for major shifts, driven by five key trends with generative AI as a central force.

The Five Trends:

  1. AI is the Centrepiece: Telcos are using AI for network optimization, customer service, and personalization, with 7% identified as "leaders" in adoption.
  2. Focus on B2C Value: Amid muted ARPU growth (3% CAGR by 2030), telcos are using Fixed-Mobile Convergence (FMC) to reduce churn and enhance loyalty.
  3. Rethinking B2B Growth: While diversifying into ICT, enterprise connectivity still accounts for ~70% of telco B2B revenue.
  4. Alternative Connectivity: Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) and Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites are showing explosive growth, with FWA connections expected to reach 350 million by 2030.
  5. Cost Optimization: Telcos continue to downsize (14% average FTE decline from 2018-2023), use AI for efficiency, and simplify products to widen margins.
