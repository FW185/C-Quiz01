Global Insights

Navigating the ICT Operating Model Dilemma for Telcos

With core connectivity revenues stagnating, telcos are entering the B2B ICT market. Success requires choosing the right operating model to balance leveraging existing assets with the agility needed for innovation.

Three Operating Models:

  • Integrated Model: ICT is part of the main telco B2B unit. Leverages brand and customer base but can be slow. Best for emerging markets with low competition. (e.g., Verizon)
  • Leveraged Model: ICT is a separate legal entity but maintains a coordinated go-to-market strategy. Fosters an entrepreneurial culture but can dilute the brand. Ideal for evolving markets. (e.g., T-Systems by Deutsche Telekom)
  • Independent Model: Semi-autonomous ICT arm that capitalizes on certain parent resources but operates independently. Best for highly competitive, innovation-driven markets. (e.g., Solutions by STC)

Europe's TMT: The New Growth Engine or Another Lost Decade?

Europe's Technology, Media, and Telecom (TMT) sector has significantly underperformed its global peers. Its share of global TMT market cap has plunged from 30% in 2000 to just 7% in 2023, representing a missed opportunity of **\$8 trillion**.

Five Key Battlegrounds for a Turnaround:

  1. Bespoke at Scale (AI & Next-Gen Software): The largest opportunity, with a projected incremental spend of **\$310 billion** by 2030.
  2. Fight for Consumer Attention (Content & Commerce): Valued at \$200 billion, challenged by fragmented audiences and global giants.
  3. Symbiotic Partnerships (Tech Services): A \$135 billion opportunity for telcos to become enabling partners in a complex tech ecosystem.
  4. Competing for Computing (Data Infrastructure): A \$110 billion market fueled by AI demand, currently dominated by global hyperscalers.
  5. Capturing and Going Beyond the Core (Connectivity): The smallest growth area at \$35 billion, focused on maximizing traditional value and expanding into adjacent services.

International News

  • SAMENA Council & WBBA: Signed an MoU to collaborate on expanding broadband access across South Asia, the Middle East, and North Africa.
  • Vodafone & Three UK Merger: Completed their £15 billion merger, creating the UK's largest mobile operator with 27 million customers and promising an £11 billion investment in nationwide 5G SA.
  • Space Norway & Starlink: Space Norway became an authorized reseller of Starlink's LEO satellite services for maritime and enterprise customers in the EMEA region.
  • T-Mobile & SailGP: Partnered to use 5G Advanced Network Solutions and AI to deliver immersive, real-time race coverage and operations for the Sail Grand Prix.
  • World Bank Forecast: Cut its 2025 global growth forecast to 2.3% due to rising tariffs and economic uncertainty.

Domestic Insights (Indonesia)

  • Telkom's AI for SMEs: Developing AI-driven solutions for MSMEs, including image recognition for product verification and CCTV analytics for retail businesses.
  • Telkom & Telkomsel Leadership Changes: **Dian Siswarini** was appointed as the new President Director of Telkom. **Diaz F.M. Hendropriyono** was named President Commissioner of Telkomsel. Concerns were raised about potential conflicts of interest as two Deputy Ministers were appointed as President Commissioners of major operators.
  • ZTE & XLSMART Partnership: The merged entity XLSMART signed a strategic partnership with ZTE to enhance digital connectivity and build a future-ready 5G network infrastructure.
  • Indosat-GoTo AI Launch: Introduced **Sahabat-AI**, a 70-billion-parameter open-source large language model supporting five local languages.
  • Telkomsel's Product Simplification: Discontinuing its **Telkomsel Lite** and Telkomsel Prabayar brands to streamline its portfolio and drive up ARPU.
  • WIFI-TLKM MoU Clarification: Telkom clarified its MoU with WIFI is to monetize underutilized capacity in existing IndiHome-covered areas through a wholesale agreement, not to expand into new areas.
