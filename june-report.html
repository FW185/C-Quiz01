Global Insights

Navigating the ICT Operating Model Dilemma for Telcos

With core connectivity revenues stagnating, telecom companies are increasingly seeking growth in the B2B Information and Communication Technology (ICT) services market. Success requires a shift in mindset from product-centric to customer-centric solutions. Telcos must choose the right operating model to balance leveraging existing synergies with fostering the independence needed for innovation.

Three Operating Models:

  • Integrated Model: The ICT business remains part of the larger telecom B2B entity. It leverages the established brand and customer base but can limit agility. This model is best for emerging markets with low competition. (e.g., Verizon)
  • Leveraged Model: Creates a separate legal entity for the ICT business, allowing greater agility while still coordinating go-to-market strategies. This model fosters an entrepreneurial culture but risks diluting the brand. Ideal for evolving markets. (e.g., T-Systems by Deutsche Telekom)
  • Independent Model: The ICT business operates semi-autonomously, capitalizing on certain resources of the parent telco while retaining the freedom to innovate rapidly. This is best for highly competitive, innovation-driven markets but can lead to resource-allocation conflicts. (e.g., Solutions by STC)

Key enablers for success in any model include a clear service scope, robust governance, aligned incentives, and fit-for-purpose processes and technology platforms.

Technology, Media, and Telecom in Europe: The New Growth Engine or Another Decade of Missing Out?

Over the past 25 years, Europe's Technology, Media, and Telecom (TMT) sector has stagnated while the global market has boomed. Europe's share of global TMT market cap has plunged from 30% to just 7%, representing a missed opportunity of **$8 trillion**.

Europe's TMT Decline

Five Key Battlegrounds for a European TMT Turnaround:

Analysis shows eight major technological transformations could generate $1.85 trillion in additional spending across Europe by 2030. Five emerging TMT sectors could fuel a comeback worth nearly $800 billion.

  1. Bespoke at Scale (AI & Next-Gen Software): The largest opportunity, with a projected incremental spend of **$310 billion** by 2030 (15% CAGR).
  2. Fight for Consumer Attention (Content & Commerce): Valued at $200 billion (4% CAGR), challenged by fragmented audiences and global giants.
  3. Symbiotic Partnerships (Tech Services): A $135 billion opportunity (5% CAGR) for telcos to become enabling partners in a complex tech ecosystem.
  4. Competing for Computing (Data Infrastructure): A $110 billion market (15% CAGR) fueled by AI demand, currently dominated by global hyperscalers.
  5. Capturing and Going Beyond the Core (Connectivity): The smallest growth area at $35 billion (2% CAGR), focused on maximizing traditional value and expanding into adjacent services.

To succeed, European TMT companies must act decisively, leveraging local strengths, advanced tech, and smart M&A to compete with global leaders.
